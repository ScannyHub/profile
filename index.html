Research

×

Surveying with RTK-GPS, setting base-stations, determining ground control points for UAV survey, long days in the field, and dealing with problems on the ground are facts of life I have been acquainted with.

The synthesis of these hurdles occurred during my time performing research in the Tarndale Valley in Gisborne, New Zealand. However, having the validation of presenting my findings at a conference of Australasian geological experts more than made up for the trouble.

The goal of the research was to test the validity of contemporary landform evolution models through numerical modelling, and to trial what was a novel methodology at the time, the use of a UAV to quickly and easily collect high-quality digital elevation models.

Conceptual landform evolution models were confirmed as valid, demonstrated was the practicable use of UAV DEM data for the rapid acquisition of high quality topographic models

Whilst flying the drone was easy enough, trekking across a gully only accessible by foot, and teetering along the edge of the scarp in order to establish ground control points took a significant amount of time and effort! An in-depth explanation can be found on page 38 of the conference schedule.
